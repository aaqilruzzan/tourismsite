Sigiriya

The Lion’s Rock, popularly known as Sigiriya, is a rock castle and palace in Sri Lanka. Gardens, ponds, and other constructions surround this ruin. King Kassapa constructed Sigiriya. UNESCO identified Sigiriya as one of the World Heritage Sites in 1982.

Big Ben

One of the most significant places to visit in UK in summer is the Big Ben. It is, in fact, the name of the clock tower which is located near the Westminster Abbey in central London. The tower, designed by Augustus Pugin, is about a hundred metres tall.

The Sagrada Familia

The Temple Expiatori de la Sagrada Família (Expiatory Temple of the Holy Family) is considered to be the symbol of Barcelona by many residents, and the one place you shouldn’t miss when you visit the Catalan capital.

The Eiffel Tower

No trip to France’s biggest city is complete without a closeup view of the Eiffel Tower’s 8,000 parts. Once you’ve admired the marvelous architecture, wander up the staircase to restaurants across multiple levels, plus wondrous views of the city itself.

Hot Air Balloon Ride in Cappadocia

One of the best places in the world for hitching a scenic hot air balloon ride, Cappadocia, turkey is famous for its picturesque 360-degree viewpoints of sprawling valleys and weird natural rock formations.

Taj Mahal

The world's most famous testimony to the power of love. Named after Mumtaz Mahal, the favorite wife of Emperor Shah Jahan, this was begun upon her death in 1631 and took 20,000 workmen until 1648 to complete.

Las Vegas Strip

Walking along the Las Vegas Strip USA, the main street leading through the city past the mega resorts, is like strolling through an amusement park for adults. Recreations of the New York skyline, the Eiffel Tower, the canals of Venice, and many more.

The Great Wall of China

"Nobody can be a true hero unless he has been on the Great Wall" goes the popular Chinese saying, one that clearly demonstrates the importance placed upon this unique ancient monument.

Colosseum

This huge amphitheater in Italy, the largest of its kind ever built by the Roman Empire and the largest of their constructions to survive, remained a model for sports facilities right up to modern times.

PHANDER LAKE

Phander Lake, located in Pakistan, is almost too good to be true. The teal-coloured lake sits silently amongst light-green trees befitting a landscape painting.

Berlin's Brandenburg Gate

Undoubtedly Berlin's most iconic structure, it's hard to believe that the majestic structure you see today was severely damaged during WWII. It was also once part of the infamous Berlin Wall.
